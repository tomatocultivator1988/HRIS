# Payroll Terms Explained (Simple)

## The Three Main Amounts

Think of it like this: You earn money, then some gets added, then some gets taken away.

---

## 1. BASIC PAY
**What it is**: Your regular salary for the days you worked (no extras yet)

**How it's calculated**:
```
For Monthly employees:
Basic Pay = (Monthly Salary ÷ Working Days) × Days Worked

Example:
Monthly Salary: ₱30,000
Working Days in April: 22 days
Days Worked: 22 days
Basic Pay = (₱30,000 ÷ 22) × 22 = ₱30,000

If you only worked 20 days:
Basic Pay = (₱30,000 ÷ 22) × 20 = ₱27,272.73
```

**Think of it as**: Your base earnings before anything else

---

## 2. GROSS PAY
**What it is**: Total earnings BEFORE deductions (Basic + All Extras)

**How it's calculated**:
```
Gross Pay = Basic Pay + Overtime + Leave Pay + Allowances + Bonuses

Example:
Basic Pay:        ₱30,000.00
Overtime Pay:     ₱2,000.00
Leave Pay:        ₱0.00
Allowances:       ₱0.00
Bonuses:          ₱0.00
─────────────────────────────
Gross Pay:        ₱32,000.00
```

**Think of it as**: Everything you earned before government and other deductions

---

## 3. NET PAY (Take-Home Pay)
**What it is**: The actual money you receive (what goes to your bank account)

**How it's calculated**:
```
Net Pay = Gross Pay - All Deductions

Example:
Gross Pay:                    ₱32,000.00

Deductions:
  - Tax:                      ₱2,500.00
  - SSS:                      ₱581.30
  - PhilHealth:               ₱450.00
  - PagIBIG:                  ₱100.00
  - Loans:                    ₱0.00
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Total Deductions:           ₱3,631.30

Net Pay:                      ₱28,368.70
```

**Think of it as**: The money you actually take home

## Simple Formula

```
BASIC PAY
    ↓
  + Overtime
  + Leave Pay
  + Allowances
  + Bonuses
    ↓
= GROSS PAY
    ↓
  - Tax
  - SSS
  - PhilHealth
  - PagIBIG
  - Loans
    ↓
= NET PAY (What you get!)
```

## Real-World Analogy

Imagine you're selling fruits:

1. **Basic Pay** = Money from selling regular fruits (your main income)
2. **Gross Pay** = Money from regular fruits + extra sales + tips
3. **Net Pay** = Gross money - rent - electricity - taxes = What you keep

## In the Payroll System

When you look at the payroll table, you'll see:

| Employee | Days | Basic Pay | Overtime | Gross | Tax | SSS | PhilHealth | PagIBIG | Total Ded. | Net Pay |
|----------|------|-----------|----------|-------|-----|-----|------------|---------|------------|---------|
| Juan     | 22   | ₱30,000   | ₱2,126   | ₱32,126 | ₱2,500 | ₱581 | ₱450 | ₱100 | ₱3,631 | ₱28,495 |

- **Basic Pay**: Regular salary for days worked
- **Gross**: Basic + Overtime (total earned)
- **Net Pay**: Gross - Deductions (take home)

---

## Why Three Different Amounts?

1. **Basic Pay** - Shows your regular earnings (important for computing benefits)
2. **Gross Pay** - Shows total earnings before deductions (used for tax computation)
3. **Net Pay** - Shows what you actually receive (what matters to employees!)

---

## Common Questions

**Q: Why is my Net Pay less than my salary?**
A: Because of mandatory deductions (tax, SSS, PhilHealth, PagIBIG)

**Q: Which amount goes to my bank?**
A: Net Pay (take-home pay)

**Q: Which amount is used for loans?**
A: Usually Gross Pay (before deductions)

**Q: Can Net Pay be higher than Basic Pay?**
A: Yes! If you have lots of overtime/bonuses and small deductions

**Q: Can Net Pay be lower than Basic Pay?**
A: Yes! If deductions are high (loans, advances, etc.)
